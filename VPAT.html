VPAT Document

Our expertise lies in accessibility driven development. Whether we are designing a website or Evaluating for a WCAG Certification we always use POUR design principals by WCAG to meets accessibility requirements.

We use the Voluntary Product Accessibility Template (VPAT), for our WCAG Certifications that follows the accessibility standards for Section 508 (U.S.), EN 301 549 (EU), and W3C/WAI WCAG. Some companies are required to fill out a VPAT before they can release a new product, others may need one to Certify that they have met the Accessibility requirements. We use the VPAT to certify that our sites are ADA compliant.

Accessibility Evaluation

An Accessibility Evaluation is a process we use to examine your site and verify that accessibility standards have been satisfied to level AA. During this process, we will crawl your site, use evaluation tools, as-well-as manually go through the site using assistive technologies. We test the site for hands-free access, vision impairedness, low-vision, color-blind, learning disabilities, deafness or low-hearing, just to name a few.

WCAG Certification

Our certification process is easy. After we complete an evaluation we use one of the four (4) VPAT documents to Certifications your site meets the required standards and is in compliance with WCAG, Sections 508, and/or EU.

  • WCAG 508 Certification: U.S. Federal accessibility standard for 508 Compliance
  • WCAG EU Certification: European Union’s Accessibility requirements for EU compliance
  • WCAG Certification: W3C/WAI’s Web Content Accessibility Guideline for WCAG compliance
  • WCAG Full Certification: Incorporates all three of the above standards for Full Compliance

WCAG Certifications contain:

  • Full Evaluation of the site with a How-To-Fi
  • Evaluation Methods used to test the product’s accessibility
  • Notes to provide additional details concerning the product tested and the evaluation methods

We provide a PDF stamped with one of our certification logos intcating the level of certifiication your site has recive. You can display the PDF on your website for download or send to users upon request.
